Primary Zone

5+ reaches per minute

The optimal interaction zone characterized by bent elbow movements. This area minimizes fatigue and reduces risk of musculoskeletal disorders.

Secondary Zone

1-5 reaches per minute

Reachable with arm extension but not full stretch. Requires sustained deltoid and rotator cuff contraction, leading to moderate fatigue over time.

Horizontal Reach 15-19"
Vertical Range 6-12"
Shoulder Angle 30-60°
Comfort Level Moderate

Ideal UI Elements:

Next Button Settings Adjustments Secondary Options Filters
Comfort:
3

Tertiary Zone

<1 reach per minute

Requires full arm extension (180°), increasing joint stress and muscle fatigue. May require posture shifts, affecting balance and increasing strain.

Horizontal Reach 19-25"
Vertical Range >12"
Shoulder Angle >60°
Comfort Level Low

Ideal UI Elements:

Terms of Service Permissions Archive Advanced Settings Rare Actions

Ergonomic Risk: Beyond Zone 3

Placing interactive elements beyond 25 inches poses significant ergonomic risk. Continuous arm elevation leads to neck and shoulder pain, increased joint stress, and rapid muscle fatigue. Interfaces positioned too high or too far require users to deviate from neutral posture, which can cause lasting discomfort and potential injury with repeated use. Always keep interactive elements within the three defined zones.

Design Principle for AR Interaction Zones

Legibility in AR requires careful attention to orientation, brightness, transparency, and color selection. Use billboarding to keep text facing users, with optimal tilt between -5° and +20°. Adapt display brightness to ambient conditions (500-10,000 nits depending on environment). Maintain opacity between 25-75% for balance between visibility and see-through capability. Prioritize blue for primary UI and warnings, use green and yellow for secondary elements, and avoid red and brown for critical information. These evidence-based guidelines ensure consistent character recognition across varying real-world conditions.
