Legibility

Ensuring characters and symbols are easy to recognize through optimal contrast, opacity, background interference, and display characteristics in augmented reality.

Foundation

Ensure Legibility

Legibility refers to "the degree to which text is easy to read." It concerns the perceptual recognition of characters, and is primarily influenced by technical and typographic presentation‚Äîsuch as contrast, opacity, background interference, and the optical characteristics of the display. Not to be confused with readability, legibility focuses on character-level recognition rather than content comprehension.

Color Guidelines

Color Robustness in AR

Colors must maintain their intended perception across varying real-world backgrounds. Robustness is determined by chromaticity shift and naming accuracy.

Blue
Blue
EXCELLENT - PRIMARY

Most robust color. Should be adopted as the primary color. Use for warnings instead of red, which proves highly unreliable in AR contexts.

Green
Green
EXCELLENT - 80% ACCURACY

High color-naming accuracy (80%) with low-to-moderate levels of shift and dispersion. Reliable for status indicators.

Yellow
Yellow
GOOD - HIGH ACCURACY

Like green, maintains high color-naming accuracy with low-to-moderate shift. Suitable for attention elements.

Red
Red
POOR - AVOID

Performed worst in testing. Should NOT be used. A red "stop" or "warning" indicator cannot be relied upon in optical see-through AR.

Brown
Brown
POOR - 5.3% ACCURACY

Exhibits severe chromaticity shift and extremely low naming accuracy (5.3%). Avoid for critical UI elements.

Purple
Purple
GOOD - MODERATE

Moderate robustness. Suitable for secondary UI elements and decorative purposes but not critical warnings.

Design Principle for AR Legibility

Legibility in AR requires careful attention to orientation, brightness, transparency, and color selection. Use billboarding to keep text facing users, with optimal tilt between -5¬∞ and +20¬∞. Adapt display brightness to ambient conditions (500-10,000 nits depending on environment). Maintain opacity between 25-75% for balance between visibility and see-through capability. Prioritize blue for primary UI and warnings, use green and yellow for secondary elements, and avoid red and brown for critical information. These evidence-based guidelines ensure consistent character recognition across varying real-world conditions.
