Introduction

The AR Guidelines provide research-grounded guidance for designing clear, comfortable, and usable interfaces in augmented reality. The focus of this guide is on translating human perceptual, cognitive, and physical limits into actionable spatial design rules.

This document is intended to support designers and developers working on real-world AR systems by defining safe, legible, and physically sustainable interface behaviors.

How this guide is structured

The guide is organized into three main parts that move from human limits, to application, to validation through use.

PART 1

Foundations

Establishes the perceptual and physiological boundaries of human vision, cognition, and physical capability. These chapters define where AR content can exist, how it can be seen, and how users can interact with it.

PART 2

Application

Translates foundational limits into concrete design patterns for common AR interface elements like menus, notifications, data overlays, and spatial controls.

PART 3

Validation

Provides testing methods and evaluation criteria to verify that interfaces meet comfort, legibility, and usability requirements in real-world conditions.

What this guide covers

All recommendations are framed for head-worn, optical see-through AR systems used in real environments.

What it does not cover

  • Platform-specific UI components or native widget libraries
  • Game design, entertainment mechanics, or gesture vocabularies
  • Hardware engineering, optics fabrication, or device manufacturing

The purpose of this guide

This guide does not prescribe visual identity or interaction styles, but defines the human limits within which any AR interface must operate. These constraints form a closed design space governed by the human visual system, cognitive capacity, and physical body—not by screens or tradition.
