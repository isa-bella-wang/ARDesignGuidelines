CHAPTER 4

Readability

Ensuring text comprehension in augmented reality through proper sizing, contrast, and spatial layout in dynamic, variable lighting conditions.

Concepts

Ensure Readability

Readability refers to "the ease with which the meaning of text can be comprehended." It is directly related to the cognitive aspects of the reading process, including the user's ability to understand multi-word content, track sentences, and maintain context during continuous reading. Readability depends on factors such as text size, line length, spatial layout, and the amount of text presented at once.

Why Readability is Hard in AR

Rather than being presented on a fixed, uniformly lit surface, AR text exists within the complexity of the real world. Its perception is dependent on the environment surrounding it—ambient lighting, motion, background textures, depth cues, and continuous user movement.

This makes readability and legibility particularly complex challenges that require careful consideration of visual angles, contrast, and text presentation strategies.

Impact

Measuring in AR

In AR, size depends on how large something is perceived, as well as how large it actually is. This is because an object's perceived size is a factor of distance. The closer an object is, the bigger it appears; the farther away, the smaller. Therefore, measurements are not in pixels or points—they are calculated in visual angle, which is how much an object takes over space on the retina.

Recommendations

Text Sizing Guidelines

In order to calculate appropriate character height in AR, it must be specified using visual angle, not physical units, because perceptual size depends on distance. This approach aligns with Microsoft HoloLens typography recommendations and findings across AR/VR readability studies. Users consistently selected angular sizes that scale with the amount of text rather than with device type or resolution.

1 Title Text

Recommended for titles and/or short one-line text. The preferred angular size is significantly larger than body text, reflecting title-level emphasis and rapid single-glance reading.

Design Principle for AR Readability

Readability in AR requires a fundamentally different approach than traditional screen-based design. Use visual angle measurements (dmm) to ensure text scales appropriately with distance. Keep title text at 27-32 dmm and body text at 16-17 dmm. Chunk text into 2-3 line segments maximum to reduce cognitive load. Prioritize high contrast above all other enhancements. By following these evidence-based guidelines, you can create AR text that remains comfortable to read across varying distances, lighting conditions, and user movement patterns.
